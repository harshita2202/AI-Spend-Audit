# Unit Economics

## What a converted lead is worth to Credex

Assume a 10-person startup buys Cursor Business at $40/seat retail = $400/mo. Credex offers 30% discount = $280/mo to the customer. Credex margin on arbitraged credits is roughly 20% = $56/mo gross margin per customer. Average contract length 8 months. LTV per customer = $56 x 8 = $448. For enterprise deals at 50+ seats LTV is $2,000-$5,000. Conservative blended LTV = $600.

## CAC by channel

| Channel | Cost | Conversions | CAC |
|---------|------|-------------|-----|
| Hacker News Show HN | $0 cash | ~8 customers | $0 |
| Twitter organic | $0 cash | ~3 customers | $0 |
| Newsletter placement | $200 time cost | ~5 customers | $40 |
| Cold outreach | $150 time cost | ~3 customers | $50 |

Blended CAC at launch: approximately $15 (mostly time, not cash spend)

## Conversion funnel

| Stage | Rate |
|-------|------|
| Visitor to audit started | 30% |
| Audit started to completed | 70% |
| Completed to email captured | 35% |
| Email to Credex consultation | 8% |
| Consultation to purchase | 25% |

Overall: 1,000 visitors → 210 completed audits → 74 emails → 6 consultations → 1.5 purchases per 1,000 visitors.

At $600 LTV and $15 CAC = 40x ROI on marketing spend.

## Path to $1M ARR in 18 months

$1M ARR requires approximately 1,667 active Credex customers at $50/mo average.

The tool drives 500 audits/month by month 6. At 35% email capture = 175 leads/month. At 8% consultation rate = 14 consultations/month. At 25% close = 3.5 new customers/month. Months 7-18 = 12 months x 3.5 = 42 customers/month net new. 42 x 12 x $600 LTV = $302k ARR from tool alone. Remaining $700k requires Credex direct sales running in parallel plus upsells from tool-sourced customers.

What must be true: tool must reach 500+ audits/month. This requires either viral coefficient above 1 from share URLs or sustained SEO traffic from AI tool comparison queries.
