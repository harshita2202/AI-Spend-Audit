# User Interviews

## Interview 1 — Graphic Moron (Designer + Developer)

### AI tools currently paying for
Uses ChatGPT and Claude regularly for design ideas, content generation, and development-related tasks.

### Thoughts on spending
Mentioned that they probably are overpaying because they are not able to fully exhaust the usage limits every month.

### How plans were chosen
Plans were selected mainly according to current work requirements and workload.

### Have they switched plans/tools before?
No major switches yet.

### What would make them trust an AI spend audit tool?
Said they would first validate the recommendations themselves before trusting the tool enough to take action.

## Interview 2 — Freelancer (Developer)

### AI tools currently paying for
Currently pays for ChatGPT, GitHub Copilot, and occasionally Claude or Cursor depending on project workload.

Estimated monthly spend is around ₹4,000–₹10,000.

### Thoughts on spending
Said there are definitely times when multiple subscriptions feel overlapping, especially when similar features exist across tools.

### How plans were chosen
Plan decisions are usually based on coding productivity, time savings, feature limits, and overall efficiency.

### Have they switched plans/tools before?
Yes. Mentioned that they have downgraded or changed subscriptions after realizing they were not fully using certain tools or found better pricing alternatives.

### What would make them trust an AI spend audit tool?
Would trust the platform more if:
- it is transparent,
- privacy-focused,
- preferably open-source,
- and gives clear data-backed recommendations instead of generic suggestions.

---

## Key Takeaways

After speaking with users, a few common patterns became clear:

- Many people subscribe to multiple AI tools with overlapping functionality.
- Users often continue paying for plans even when they are not fully utilizing them.
- Trust is extremely important for financial recommendations.
- People are more likely to act if recommendations are transparent and data-driven.
- Simplicity and clear savings breakdowns matter more than advanced AI features.
