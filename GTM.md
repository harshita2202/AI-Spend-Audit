# Go-to-market plan

## Exact target user

Not "startups." The specific person is:

**Engineering Manager or CTO at a seed-to-Series A startup (10–50 
people), who manages a team of 4–12 developers, has budget 
responsibility for tooling, and is vaguely aware their AI spend has 
grown but hasn't audited it.** They make $120k–$180k, care deeply 
about developer productivity, and feel mild guilt every time they 
approve another AI tool subscription without a clear ROI story.

Secondary user: solo founder or indie developer who pays for 3+ 
AI tools personally and suspects they're doubling up.

## What they Google right before wanting this

- "cursor vs github copilot 2026 which is better"
- "is chatgpt team worth it for small team"
- "how much should a startup spend on ai tools per developer"
- "claude team vs pro plan difference"
- "reduce saas costs startup"

## Where they hang out online

- **r/ExperiencedDevs** and **r/cscareerquestions** — engineering 
  managers lurk and post here
- **r/startups** — founders discussing tooling costs
- **Hacker News** — Show HN posts get real feedback from this exact 
  audience
- **Indie Hackers** community (indiehackers.com/discuss) — founders 
  who track every dollar
- **Software engineers Slack groups:** Locally grown dev Slacks, 
  alumni Slacks from bootcamps
- **X/Twitter lists:** "YC founders," "indie hackers" — these 
  accounts retweet cost-saving tools regularly

## Getting the first 100 users in 30 days, $0 budget

**Week 1 — Hacker News Show HN post.**  
Title: "Show HN: I built a free tool to audit your AI tool spend 
(found $300/mo I was wasting)"  
Post on a Tuesday morning, 9am ET. Include a personal story about 
finding overspend. Respond to every comment within 2 hours. This 
alone can drive 500–2000 visitors. Target: 40 signups.

**Week 1 — Post the audit results of your own stack on X.**  
"Ran our team's AI spend through the tool I built. We were paying 
for 8 Cursor seats when 5 of our team members barely use it. $120/mo 
gone. [screenshot of results page]"  
The results page is designed to be screenshotted. This is the 
viral loop.

**Week 2 — Cold DM 20 CTOs on X/LinkedIn.**  
Not a pitch — ask for feedback. "Hey, I built this free tool, 
would love 5 minutes of your reaction. Here's the link."  
Response rate on genuine feedback asks is ~20–30%. Target: 
5 conversations → 3 actual audits run.

**Week 3 — Post in r/ExperiencedDevs and r/startups.**  
"We built a free tool that audits your team's AI tool spend — 
here's what we learned from the first 50 audits." Include 
anonymized aggregate data (average team wastes $X/mo). 
Community loves data posts.

**Week 4 — Reach out to 5 developer-focused newsletters.**  
TLDR, Console.dev, Pointer, Bytes.dev. Offer to write a guest 
post about "right-sizing your AI tool stack." Include the tool 
naturally. One newsletter placement = 200–500 targeted visitors.

## The unfair distribution channel

Credex already has relationships with companies that oversold 
AI credits. Those companies have lists of customers who bought 
credits and may now be overspending on retail plans. Credex can 
email those lists with "free audit" as the hook — this is a 
warm audience that already trusts Credex and already has a 
demonstrated interest in AI cost optimization.

No other tool has this. It's the moat.
